Building a Dark Mode Blog

January 17, 2025

Dark mode has become more than just a trend - it's a necessity for many developers and readers who spend hours looking at screens. Today, let's explore how to create a beautiful dark-themed blog using only CSS.

Why Dark Mode?

Beyond looking sleek and modern, dark mode offers real benefits:

  • Reduced eye strain in low-light environments
  • Better battery life on OLED screens
  • Enhanced focus on content
  • Professional, modern aesthetic

Color Palette Selection

The key to a good dark theme isn't just inverting colors. You need carefully chosen shades that work together:

:root {
  --bg-primary: #0f0f0f;
  --bg-secondary: #1a1a1a;
  --text-primary: #ffffff;
  --text-secondary: #ccc;
  --text-muted: #666;
  --accent: #4a9eff;
}

Notice how we avoid pure black (#000000) for the background. This reduces contrast and is easier on the eyes.

Typography Considerations

Text needs special attention in dark mode:

  1. Use slightly lower contrast than pure white on black
  2. Increase line-height for better readability
  3. Consider slightly lighter font weights
  4. Ensure adequate spacing between elements

Creating Visual Hierarchy

In dark mode, you can't rely on shadows as much. Instead, use:

.card {
  background: var(--bg-secondary);
  border: 1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.1);
  border-radius: 8px;
}

Subtle borders and background color variations create depth without harsh contrasts.

Syntax Highlighting

Code blocks need special attention in dark themes. Choose colors that are vibrant enough to distinguish syntax but not so bright they hurt the eyes:

pre {
  background: #1a1a1a;
  border: 1px solid #333;
  border-radius: 8px;
  padding: 20px;
  overflow-x: auto;
}

Testing Your Dark Theme

Always test your dark theme in different conditions:

  • Bright daylight (increase contrast if needed)
  • Complete darkness (reduce brightness)
  • Different screen types (OLED vs LCD)
  • With night shift/flux enabled

Remember, a good dark theme should be comfortable to read for extended periods. If your eyes feel strained after 10 minutes, adjust your colors.
